01Overview

A pipeline managed entirely through email

From brief submission to final regulatory sign-off, a new product passes through five phases across six cross-functional teams — all coordinated via email threads, personal spreadsheets, and verbal approvals. Nothing is tracked. Nothing is visible. Nothing is standard.

The PLM project was commissioned to understand this broken workflow end-to-end, find exactly where it fails, and design a digital platform to replace it — starting with the highest-impact pain point: the brief submission and approval flow.

02Research Approach

Watching the work as it actually happens

Why Contextual Observation

Enterprise users consistently describe ideal workflows — not actual ones. In a domain as complex as FMCG R&D, with regulatory compliance, SAP dependencies, and cross-lab coordination, it was critical to observe work in context. Contextual observation removed the gap between reported and real behaviour.

What Was Observed
  • How briefs were submitted and reviewed in practice
  • How the product manager tracked project status across teams
  • Where email became the default coordination tool
  • How revision cycles and version control were handled
  • How formulation sheets and trial reports were stored
  • How sensory evaluation feedback was collected and actioned

04Pain Points — All Phases

Every phase had critical gaps

Across all five lifecycle phases the pattern was consistent: documents manually managed, decisions over email, nothing traceable. The system wasn't broken in one place — it was held together by individual effort everywhere.

Brief Submission

No standard brief format

Marketing submitted briefs in varying formats with no enforced structure, leading to incomplete information and avoidable back-and-forth before review could even begin.

Brief Submission

No visibility after submission

Once a brief was submitted, marketing had no way to track where it sat in the pipeline. Status updates depended entirely on whoever they could reach by email or phone.

Brief Review

Reviewer ownership was unclear

No formal assignment of who reviewed what. Senior members reviewed inconsistently — some gave detailed feedback, others one-liners — creating uneven revision cycles.

Brief Revision

Revision cycles were untracked

Multiple versions of the same brief circulated over email with no versioning. Teams regularly worked from outdated documents unknowingly.

Prototype Development

Formulation data maintained individually

Formulation sheets, ingredient records, and trial data were kept in personal files. Institutional knowledge was siloed or lost when team members left.

Prototype Development

Sensory evaluation feedback was informal

Sensory reports were manually maintained with no structured form. Feedback was not standardised, making revision cycles hard to action systematically.

Stability

Stability documents managed ad hoc

TRFs, protocols, COAs, and external lab reports all tracked via email. Report compilation was a time-consuming manual exercise for every submission.

Plant Trials & Documentation

No online approval system anywhere

Plant trial approvals, machinability sign-offs, and final documentation — BOM, MFC, specs — handled through email. No formal approval timestamps or records.

06Key Insights

Four insights that shaped the design direction

INSIGHT 01

Email is doing the job of a system

Across every phase, email was simultaneously task manager, document repository, approval tool, and notification system. This is not a communication problem — it's a missing infrastructure problem. Any solution must replace email's role in the workflow, not just complement it.

INSIGHT 02

Status invisibility creates parallel conversations

Because no one could see where a project stood, teams constantly created side conversations to get updates. This doubled the communication burden and meant decisions were made without full context. Real-time status visibility is the core need.

INSIGHT 03

The Product Manager is a single point of failure

The person managing PLM held the entire pipeline together through personal knowledge and effort alone. Resource allocation, coordination, and deadline tracking all depended on one person's memory. The system needs to distribute visibility, not concentrate it.

INSIGHT 04

Institutional knowledge walks out the door

Past formulations, trial outcomes, benchmarking reports, and ingredient decisions were stored in personal files and inboxes. New projects reinvented approaches already explored — wasting time and losing knowledge every time someone left the team.

07Design Opportunity

Framing the design challenge

How Might We

"How might we design a unified PLM portal that gives every team real-time visibility into project status, standardises the brief-to-approval workflow, and preserves institutional knowledge — so no project depends on any single person's memory?"

📋

Structured Brief Submission

Standardised form with required fields eliminating incomplete briefs and removing the first source of project delays.

🔍

Live Status Tracker

Every team sees where their project stands in real time — no more status-update emails or calls to the product manager.

✅

Online Approval Workflows

Digital approvals with timestamps replacing verbal sign-offs and email confirmations across all five phases.

🗂️

Centralised Knowledge Base

Past projects, formulations, and trial data available to all — so every new project builds on the last.
